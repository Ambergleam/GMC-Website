Platforms

Android is a mobile phone operating system developed by Google. It is unique because Google is actively developing the platform and providing it at no cost to hardware manufacturers and phone carriers who want to use Android on their devices.

The iPhone is a smartphone that combines the features of a mobile phone, wireless Internet device, and iPod into one device. The multi-touch screen allows an interface based on touch, rather than a keyboard, mouse, or stylus.

Windows Mobile is a compact operating system combined with a suite of basic applications for mobile devices such as Smartphones. It is designed with similar features and the look and feel of the desktop version of Microsoft Windows.

BlackBerry is a smartphone primarily known for its ability to send and receive e-mail wherever it can access a wireless network. While including applications, such as calendar, it also supports mobile telephone, text messaging, and Web browsing.
